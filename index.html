Itauguá, Paraguay

Paraguay is a landlocked country in the heart of South America, bordered by Argentina, Brazil, and Bolivia. Known for its rich cultural heritage, Paraguay is home to a unique blend of indigenous and Spanish influences. The country is famous for its Guarani language, traditional harp music, and colorful crafts. With vast forests, rivers, and the impressive Iguazu Falls nearby, Paraguay boasts a diverse ecosystem. It is also one of the world’s largest producers of hydroelectric power, thanks to the Itaipu Dam on the Paraná River.
